Huy Hieu Pham

I am a staff Research Scientist at Vingroup Big Data Institute (VinBDI) and an Assistant Professor in the College of Engineering and Computer Science at VinUniversity, where I work on Computer Vision, Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Medical Imaging. I received my Ph.D. degree in Computer Science at the Toulouse Computer Science Research Institute (IRIT) and Toulouse Cerema Research Center, France. My research focuses on Computer Vision, Machine/Deep Learning. In particular, I design and optimize high-performance deep learning models for solving the security problems in public transport, e.g. the problem of human action recognition and behavior analytics in videos. My research works were advised by Dr. HDR. Louahdi Khoudour, Dr. Alain Crouzil, Dr. Pablo Zegers, and Prof. Sergio A. Velastin.

I spent half a year at the ICA laboratory, École des Mines d'Albi under the supervision of Prof. Jean-José Orteu for working on a research project. My work was part of a dynamic multi-partner robotized airplane inspection project, called Air-Cobot, lead by AKKA Technologies and AIRBUS group.

Previously, I did my bachelors at the Hanoi University of Science and Technology (HUST). My graduate internship was done at International Research Institute MICA, Hanoi, Vietnam et AGIM laboratory, Université Grenoble Alpes, Grenoble, France. The main goal of this project is to develop a computer vision algorithm based on electrode matrix and mobile Kinect for detecting obstacles.

Research

I'm interested in Image Processing, Computer Vision, Machine Learning, Statistics, and Optimization. Much of my research is about understanding the physical world (shape, depth, motion, object detection and recognition) from images and videos. I have also worked in Medical Imaging projects.
